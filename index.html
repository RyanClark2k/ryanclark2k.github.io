Work Experience

Below I summarize my software engineering work experience thus far. You can also download my resume or view my LinkedIn at https://www.linkedin.com/in/ryan-clark2k/

  • Full Stack Software Engineering Intern · Uncountable · March 2021 - September 2021
    • At Uncountable, I built a no-code tool that engineers, account managers, and customers use to configure data uploads into the company's cloud platform. Previously, every time a customer wanted to extract data from a new file format, an engineer would have to write custom code to parse the file and restructure the data. This process could take weeks of back-and-forth with the customer before getting it right. With my system, this work can now be done in minutes in most cases, and usually an engineer does not even need to be involved. In the last month of my internship, more than 5,400 files were uploaded and processed using the infrastructure I worked on. In addition to my main project, I also worked on sales demos that helped close several large deals, contributed to core platform features, and closed dozens of support tickets relating to database optimization, UX improvements, and bug fixes.
  • Software Engineering Intern · Western Asset Management · June 2020 - September 2021
    • I worked as a software engineering intern for three months, and as a part-time contractor for one month, for the Risk Management department of Western Asset Management, a global asset management firm. My primary project at Western Asset was to design an internal tool using React and Julia for calculating risk metrics of portfolios and subdivisions of portfolios. The tool was a great success and became widely adopted by dozens of risk managers, as the efficient numeric algorithm I implemented reduced the time to analyze a portfolio from 20 minutes to under 2 minutes. Additionally, due to efficient caching I was able to update the calculations in seconds, allowing them to simulate trades and reprocess the data in real time.
  • Research Assistant · Caltech Emotion and Social Cognition Lab · June 2019 - September 2021
    • I worked with Professor Damian Stanley (visiting from Adelphi University) in Caltech's Emotion and Social Cognition Lab to develop models of Implicit Cognitive Bias. We analyzed a database of Implicit Association Test (IAT) results to model the relationship between Implicit and Explicit bias.
  • Software Engineer · Caltech Department of Geology · October 2018 - July 2019
    • Contributed to development of a Python interface to the alphaMELTS computational petrology program for Caltech Department of Geology. Developed visualization tools for the Python port.

I led weekly office hours and graded sets and exams for a class of over 100 students. Later that year in Fall 2021 I was a TA for ACM 104: Applied Linear Algebra.
